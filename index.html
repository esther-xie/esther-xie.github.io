One of the goals of my study is to understand how design as a discipline deals with fluidity. The worldâ€™s attitudes towards fluidity are inherited from colonialism, where regulation of fluidity was employed as spatial statecraft. Fluidity has been associated with discordance and often provoked trepidation, signified by the collective uncertainty towards the sea-level rise and the (fluid) pandemic. I want to explore, using tools of digital media, urban design, and landscape architecture, whether a new framework could be adopted to embrace fluidity in the future.
